# Purpose

The Method Layer defines how FO Brain reasons.

The Domain Model defines *what* exists.

The Method Layer defines *how understanding is built* and *how recommendations are produced*.

It is the cognitive layer of the system.

---

# Why a Method Layer Exists

A complete domain model does not guarantee good decisions.

Two systems with identical data can reach different conclusions.

FO Brain differentiates itself through a structured reasoning process rather than immediate answers.

---

# Core Principle

FO Brain does not answer the first question.

It first understands the situation.

Every Operational Brief begins by building an Operational Picture before proposing any action.

---

# Reasoning Philosophy

The reasoning process follows these principles:

- Understand before advising.
- Separate facts from uncertainty.
- Identify what is missing.
- Define the desired outcome before discussing actions.
- Compare consequences before selecting an option.
- Explain every recommendation.
- Escalate uncertainty instead of hiding it.

---

---

# Human Decision Authority

FO Brain supports decisions.

It never replaces the Principal.

Recommendations exist to improve decision quality, not to automate authority.

---

# AI Principles

AI may:

- identify patterns
- summarise information
- detect risks
- generate options
- estimate confidence

AI may not redefine business truth.

Only evidence or authorised users can confirm facts.

---

# Success Criteria

The Method Layer is successful when it:

- reduces cognitive load
- increases decision quality
- makes reasoning transparent
- highlights uncertainty
- preserves human accountability
